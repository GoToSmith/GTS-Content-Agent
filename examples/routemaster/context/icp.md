# Reader Persona - RouteMaster

## Primary Reader Profile

- **Role:** Operations Manager or Fleet Manager at a last-mile delivery company
- **Company:** Delivery, courier, or logistics company with 50-500 vehicles, operating in urban/suburban Europe
- **Knowledge level:** Intermediate. Knows delivery operations deeply but may be new to AI-powered optimization tools
- **How they find articles:** Google search during work hours, LinkedIn posts shared by peers, industry newsletters (FleetOwner, Logistics Manager), forwarded links from colleagues

## Secondary Readers (Buying Committee)

- **CFO / Finance Director** - reads to understand ROI, payback period, and total cost of ownership
- **COO / General Manager** - reads to evaluate strategic fit and scalability as the fleet grows
- **IT Manager** - reads to check integration requirements, API compatibility, data security
- **Dispatch Supervisor** - reads to understand daily workflow impact and learning curve for the team

How this affects content: Every commercial-intent article needs an ROI section with specific numbers. Keep technical depth but include a summary box for non-technical stakeholders. Address integration concerns proactively in relevant articles.

## Assumed Knowledge (what we don't need to explain)

- Understands what route planning is and why it matters
- Familiar with basic fleet operations (dispatch, driver management, delivery windows)
- Knows their own cost structure (fuel, labor, vehicle maintenance)
- Not necessarily familiar with AI/ML concepts, optimization algorithms, or SaaS evaluation frameworks

## Main Pain Points and Frustrations

1. "We spend 2 hours every morning planning routes in Excel and they're already wrong by 10am"
2. "Our fuel costs went up 30% this year and I can't figure out where to cut"
3. "Drivers keep missing time windows and customers are complaining"
4. "We added 20 vehicles but deliveries per driver actually went down"
5. "Every route optimization tool I've looked at is built for enterprise fleets with 2,000+ trucks. We have 80."
6. "I tried free route planners but they can't handle time windows or vehicle capacity limits"

## Purchase Triggers

- A month where fuel costs spike unexpectedly
- Customer complaints about missed delivery windows hitting a threshold
- A competitor delivering faster with a similar fleet size
- The company wins a new contract that requires 40% more daily deliveries
- A new operations manager joins from a company that already uses route optimization
- Board or investor pressure to improve unit economics before the next funding round

## What Convinces Them

- Case studies from similar-sized fleets in their industry, with specific cost savings numbers
- ROI calculators they can fill in with their own data
- Side-by-side comparisons of manual planning vs. optimized routes (with real metrics)
- Short implementation timelines (weeks, not months)
- Free trial or pilot program to prove value before committing
- Testimonials from operations managers (not just C-level quotes)

## What Repels Them

- Claims of "90% cost reduction" without evidence
- Content clearly written for enterprise logistics (irrelevant to a 100-vehicle fleet)
- Pricing hidden behind "contact sales" with no ballpark
- Case studies from unrelated industries (manufacturing, retail) instead of delivery
- Overly academic content about optimization algorithms when they need practical guidance

## Content Consumption Habits

- **Primary channels:** Google search during work hours, LinkedIn feed, FleetOwner newsletter, Supply Chain Digital
- **Preferred formats:** Actionable how-to articles (under 10 min read), comparison tables, ROI calculators, short case studies
- **Reading context:** At desk between dispatch shifts, on tablet during warehouse walks, on phone during commute
- **Communities and forums:** LinkedIn groups for logistics professionals, Reddit r/logistics, Fleet Management Weekly newsletter
- **Trusted publications:** FleetOwner, Logistics Manager Magazine, Supply Chain Digital, Transport Topics
- **Newsletter behavior:** Subscribes to 2-3 industry newsletters, opens only when subject line mentions cost savings or fleet optimization
