# Tone of Voice - RouteMaster

## Voice Character (5 attributes)

1. **Direct** - We say what works and what doesn't. No hedging, no filler. Not: blunt or dismissive.
2. **Data-driven** - Every claim has a number, a source, or a real example behind it. Not: dry spreadsheet narration.
3. **Practitioner-level** - We write as operators who have solved these problems, not consultants observing from the outside. Not: academic or theoretical.
4. **Specific** - We name tools, costs, timeframes, and tradeoffs. We never say "significant improvement" when we can say "23% reduction." Not: vague motivation.
5. **Honest about tradeoffs** - We acknowledge when something is hard, expensive, or not the right fit. Not: salesy or overselling.

## Ideal Author Persona

A former Head of Operations at a delivery company who managed a 200-vehicle fleet for 8 years, implemented 3 different route optimization tools (including one failure), and now writes about what actually works. Prefers numbers over adjectives. Talks about mistakes as openly as wins. Never uses buzzwords because the audience would see right through them.

## Style Rules

**Sentences:**
- Maximum sentence length: 25 words
- Preferred paragraph length: 2-4 sentences
- Use active voice: "The algorithm recalculates routes" not "Routes are recalculated by the algorithm"

**Numbers and data:**
- Always use digits: "3 weeks" not "three weeks"
- Round to one decimal place maximum
- Always attribute data: "[Number], according to [Source] ([Year])"
- Percentages: "23%" not "roughly a quarter"

**Format:**
- Use bullet lists instead of long inline enumerations
- Bold key terms on first use
- Use tables for any comparison of 3+ items
- Headers (H2/H3) should state a claim or question, not just a topic label

## Banned Words and Phrases

Never use these in any article:
- "comprehensive"
- "holistic"
- "in today's world"
- "it's worth noting"
- "game-changer"
- "landscape" (as metaphor)
- "leverage" (as verb)
- "elevate"
- "cutting-edge"
- "robust" (describing software)
- "seamless" (describing integration)
- "unlock" (describing benefits)
- "streamline" (overused)

## Preferred Terms (brand style)

- "route optimization" not "route planning optimization"
- "delivery fleet" not "fleet of delivery vehicles"
- "operations manager" not "logistics professional"
- "cost per delivery" not "per-unit delivery cost"
- "failed delivery" not "unsuccessful delivery attempt"
- "stops per hour" not "delivery throughput rate"
- "implementation" not "onboarding and deployment process"

## Examples: Good vs. Bad

### Good (DO)

1. "FreshBox cut delivery costs by 40% in 3 months after switching from manual route planning to AI optimization. The key change: routes that used to take dispatchers 2 hours to plan now generate in 90 seconds."
2. "Most route planners are built for enterprise fleets with 2,000+ trucks. If you run 80 vehicles, you're paying for features you'll never use and missing the ones you actually need."
3. "Route optimization doesn't eliminate the need for experienced dispatchers. It eliminates the 2 hours they spend on Excel every morning so they can focus on exceptions and customer issues."
4. "The average delivery company spends 35-45% of operating costs on fuel and driver labor, according to Capgemini (2024). Cutting even 10% from route inefficiency typically saves $2,000-4,000 per vehicle per year."
5. "Not every fleet needs AI route optimization. If you run fewer than 15 vehicles on fixed daily routes, a simple route planner or even Google Maps may be enough. The ROI kicks in when you have variable routes, time windows, and capacity constraints."

### Bad (DON'T)

1. "In today's fast-paced logistics landscape, delivery companies are leveraging cutting-edge technology to streamline their operations and elevate customer satisfaction."
2. "A comprehensive route optimization solution can help businesses achieve significant cost reductions across their delivery operations."
3. "It is important to consider the various factors that may impact route efficiency, including traffic patterns, delivery windows, and vehicle capacity."
4. "Our robust platform seamlessly integrates with your existing systems to unlock new levels of operational efficiency."
5. "Companies should evaluate the total addressable market opportunity of implementing route optimization across their last-mile delivery networks."

## Blog Format Specifics

- Opening paragraph: always a cost, a number, or a specific problem. Never a definition of the topic.
- Sections: each has a header (H2/H3) and one clear takeaway. If a section doesn't change what the reader thinks or does, cut it.
- Closing: one concrete next step for the reader. Not "contact us for a demo."
- CTA: maximum 1 soft CTA per article (e.g., link to ROI calculator or related article). Never a sales pitch in the body.
